🔬 Research Overview

Recent calls for green and sustainable manufacturing have prompted a trend towards biosynthesis utilizing nature's catalysts, enzymes. While enzymes facilitate many biochemical reactions crucial for life processes, their reactivity lags significantly behind that of chemocatalysts, thereby limiting the scope of biomanufacturing. We focus on crafting versatile biocatalysts with tailored functionalities that are both new-to-nature and challenging to achieve through chemocatalysis. Specifically, we employ visible-light catalysis to introduce highly reactive chemical intermediates, unlocking new reactivity in enzymes. Additionally, we utilize directed evolution to reshape protein structures, fine-tuning both reactivity and selectivities. Recently, we repurposed (1) flavoproteins to function as visible-light-excitable enzymes, enabling enantioselective redox-neutral radical hydroarylations; and (2) thiamine-dependent lyases to serve as radical acyl transferases, achieving unprecedented asymmetric radical-radical cross couplings.
